Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about treatments, recovery timelines, and consulting details.

You should consult an orthopedic specialist if you experience persistent joint or bone pain lasting more than a week, significant swelling, difficulty walking or standing, severe back pain radiating down your legs (sciatica), or any acute musculoskeletal injury or fracture.

Spinal pain management begins with conservative protocols including target physiotherapy, custom posture correction, and targeted pain therapeutics. For structural issues like herniated discs or canal stenosis failing non-surgical care, micro-discectomy and keyhole spinal decompression options are available for quick recovery.

During a total knee or hip replacement, the damaged cartilage and bone ends of the joint are carefully resurfaced and replaced with premium, bio-compatible implants made of metal and advanced polyethelene. This eliminates friction and bone-on-bone contact, providing substantial pain relief and restored mobility.
